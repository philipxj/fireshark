How to use your phone as a Sony Bravia TV remote

Lost the remote down the sofa? Kids snapped the buttons? Batteries dead at the worst time? You don't need to buy a replacement — your iPhone or Android phone can become a full Sony Bravia remote in under a minute, for free.

Short answer: Yes — if your Sony Bravia runs Android TV or Google TV and is on Wi-Fi, you can control it entirely from your phone. No physical remote required, no extra hardware to buy.

What you'll need

  • A Sony Bravia smart TV running Android TV or Google TV (most 2016 and newer models).
  • Your phone and TV on the same Wi-Fi network.
  • The free SonLink app — built specifically for Sony Bravia.

Do it in 3 steps

  1. 1

    Connect to the same Wi-Fi

    Put your phone on the same Wi-Fi network as your TV. (Not the guest network — they can't see each other.)

  2. 2

    Turn on network remote control

    On the Bravia, open Settings → Network → Home network setup and enable remote / network control. This lets your phone talk to the TV.

  3. 3

    Install SonLink and pair

    Open SonLink, pick your TV from the auto-discovered list, and confirm the code that appears on screen. That's it — you've got a full remote in your hand.

Lost or broken remote? Here's why your phone is the better fix

Ordering a genuine Sony replacement remote means days of waiting and HK$150–400 for a part you'll lose again. A universal remote needs pairing codes and rarely supports every Bravia button. Your phone is already in your pocket — and it gives you features a plastic remote can't:

  • Type with a real keyboard — search YouTube or sign into apps without pecking letters one by one on screen.
  • Trackpad mode for smooth menu navigation in Google TV.
  • Jump straight into apps — Netflix, YouTube, Disney+ in one tap.
  • Never lose it — and never replace a battery.

The TV doesn't show up — what now?

Almost always one of these:

  • Different networks. Confirm the phone isn't on a guest or 5GHz/2.4GHz split network the TV can't reach.
  • Network control is off. Re-check Settings → Network → Home network setup on the Bravia.
  • TV is fully asleep. Wake it with the power button on the set once, then try pairing.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use my phone as a Sony Bravia TV remote?

Yes. If your Bravia runs Android TV or Google TV and is on Wi-Fi, SonLink gives you a full remote on your iPhone or Android phone — power, volume, navigation and apps.

Do I need the original remote to set it up?

No. As long as the TV is already on your Wi-Fi and network remote control is enabled, you can pair and control everything from your phone.
